Are tropical rainforests a carbon source or a carbon sink?

Yujie Wang, 2017-11-29

Funny algorithm

There was a recent research published on "Science" about the carbon cycle in tropical ranforest ( DOI). The authors argued that the tropical rainforests were a carbon source from aboveground observations from 2003 to 2014. I have to say "Where is the logic?"

In their calculations, the gain of carbon is the forest growth, and the cost is the deforestation or plant density loss caused by disturblance. In brief, the algorithm is to compare the total carbon amount of current state versus the total carbon amount of the last state. What a simple and "logical" calculation!

According to their algorithm, Arabic countries are the number one carbon source. First, you calculate the total solid or liquid carbon of last year. Second, you calculate the total solid or liquid carbon of this year. Or you don't have to calculate all at, just check the oil market information! Is this fair?

Or we may think in another scenario, we have a big forest that is used for wood production. We cut down trees every year to make furniture or to make fire at a reasonable speed. The total carbon of the forest is constant every year. So? Forest is not carbon sink or source. We, the human beings, are using the furniture that we did not own last year. We "creat" carbon, we are the carbon sink! What an amazing world!

What is carbon source and what is carbon sink

The definitions of carbon source and sink in ecology refer to the source and sink of gaseous CO2. For example, human beings breathe out CO2 means human beings are carbon source; a tree photosynthesizes and uptakes CO2 means the tree is a carbon sink. As to whether an eco-system is a carbon source or a carbon sink depends on the balance of gaseous phase CO2. In this case, if a man compressed the CO2 he breathes to a tank and never releases it back, then he is not a source; if a man compressed more CO2 than he breathes into a tank, then he can be a sink. As to the eco-system, where there is no solid or liquid phase CO2 exchange, the total gain would be the photosynthesis, and the total gain will be the total respiration. Therefore, the computation used in the science paper was wrong!

The carbon source definition used by those authors is in the solid phase carbon but not gaseous phase. Then it becomes clear that the tropical rainforests are a source of wood, and it is also clear that the human world is the sink of wood. Any of the above arguments are right, but people should recognize the definitons of carbon source and carbon sink.
